Pod Structure & Roles

Pod Leader

Acts as the lead for the pod, facilitates communication, and delegates tasks during unplanned absences.

Primary Owner

Has day-to-day responsibility for their assigned accounts and maintains the "Living Document".

Primary Backup

Has deep familiarity with the accounts and serves as the main point of contact during an absence.

The Rest of the Pod

Serve as general support for all accounts within the pod and can be consulted as needed.

Minimum Viable Information

For a backup to be effective, they must have easy access to the following key pieces of information. It is the Primary Owner's responsibility to ensure these are up-to-date.

Gong Recordings

Access to 'Get Briefed' and other relevant call recordings.

Named Contacts

Key customer contacts clearly listed in Gainsight and SFDC.

Current Fires

Documented in the Action Plan field of the account's success plan.

Critical Jiras

P0/P1 tickets must include context for the backup to understand the situation.

Active Initiatives

Any projects with deadlines occurring during a planned absence.

Activating a Backup: The Hand-Off Process

For Planned Absences (PTO)

A proactive hand-off ensures a smooth transition before scheduled time off.

  1. Inform the Pod: Post OOO dates and links to relevant "Living Documents" or Success Plans in the Pod's Slack channel.
  2. Update Systems: Set your OOO on Gmail to direct inquiries to the Pod Google Group, and on Slack to name the specific Primary Backup for key accounts.
  3. Brief if Needed (Optional): Schedule a quick call with the Pod if complex issues require a verbal hand-off.

For Unplanned Absences

A clear protocol for when team members are unexpectedly out of office.

  1. Manager Communicates: The manager informs the Pod Leader that a team member is unexpectedly OOO.
  2. Pod Leader Delegates: The Pod Leader reviews the absent TAM's calendar and delegates urgent tasks (ticket reviews, customer calls, etc.) to the Primary Backup.
  3. Backup Communicates: The Primary Backup informs the account's named contacts about the temporary absence to manage expectations.

Tools, Documents & When to Use Them

"Living Document"

What: A centralized document (Google Doc, Confluence page) with all key account info.

When: Maintained continuously. It's the first place a backup should look for information.

Gong

What: Call recording and intelligence platform.

When: Used by backups to get up to speed on recent customer conversations via 'Get Briefed'.

Gainsight/SFDC

What: Customer success and CRM platforms.

When: Used to find Named Contacts and review Success Plans, especially the Action Plan for current issues.

Pod Slack Channel

What: A private channel for internal pod communication.

When: Used for daily updates, quick questions, and for the Primary TAM to announce a planned absence.

Pod Google Group

What: An external-facing email alias for the pod.

When: Used in the Primary TAM's Gmail OOO message to catch external inquiries during an absence.
